Vela Responsive App

Introduction

  • Duration: 10 months
  • Role: UX/UI Designer, Researcher
  • Tools: Pen & paper, UsabilityHub and Figma

Background

This is a project I completed as a part of a CareerFoundry UX Design course.

About- Vela is primarily a weather tracking app, a better way of monitoring weather data to water sport enthusiasts to ensure they are as safe as possible while doing what they love.

Problem Statement- Users who is a beginner at water sports, need a one stop shop App where it provide clear and easy to understand data of wind, wave and weather conditions visually can help them avoid dangerous situations and better prepare for a day of sailing, diving, or surfing.

User Research

Research Goals
  • Determine if users plan their water activities, if they do plan ahead how they dose it. Do they have any preference while doing it.
  • Discover pain point and difficulties users may face operating weather forecasting apps.
  • Determine what weather information users need and what types of tasks users perform.
  • What are individuals motivations and frustrations when surrounding their sport/hobby?

Interviews

Tool

Zoom, Google meet

Participants

3 users who are participantd of at leat one water sport

Key Insights

  • Struggling with data- Users often feel that existing platforms lack sufficient support to help them analyze weather data effectively.
  • Planning trips- Water sports enthusiasts dedicate significant effort to planning their activities, expecting a corresponding value in return.
  • Diversity- Many water sports enthusiasts engage in multiple water sports, yet current weather apps are either too complex for beginners or designed to one specific sports, lacking adaptability to diverse user needs.
  • Top 3 most useful features
    • Safe location feature
    • Weather alert feature
    • compare weather feature

User Personas

Tyler

Tyler is a 26 year old business analyst living in Melbourne. She work in a local business about 20 minutes away from her apartment, She loves outdoor activities and fell in love with surfing recently. As a beginner Tyler often find difficult understanding the data provided on the apps out in the market.

Dave

Dave is 46 year old business owner who runs a dive shop at Florida, USA. He is father of two sons, as the main source of income for his family, he is dedicated to work hard. To be able to access to most accurate weather forecast means he can plan trips for his shop. Therefor having a reliable source of weather information is important for him.

User Flows

Flow #1: Save location

Dave(Secondary Persona) "Got to make a reasonable plan for my customers so they have a fun and safe trip"

Scenario: Dave is making the plan for dive trip tomorrow, he need to choose the best spot among various usual sites.

Flow #2: Add alert

Tyler(primarily Persona)

"I wonder what the weather will be like for this weekend. hopefully I can go on surfing"

Scenario: Tyler is at her lunch break from work, she wants to quickly check the weather conditions to see if it's suitable for her to go on surfing this weekend

Flow #3: Compare weather

Tyler(primarily Persona)

"I want to explore the surf spots near by me, then compare the weather conditions to make decision on where to go "

Scenario: Tyler has decided to go on surfing whis weekend but not sure which spot best match her skill level.

User Testing

Click here for the full plan, script & report

Goals

The overall goal of this study is to test the learnability and possible errors of the mobile version of the app’s main features. I will observe and measure if users understand the purpose/value of the app, its navigation and how to complete the main functions.

Test Objectives

  • What are participants’ reactions to the concept of the app?
  • Do participants see value in the features of the app?
  • Are participants able to successfully browse and read a blog article?
  • Are participants able to successfully locate and practice an exercise?
  • Do participants make errors? Are participants able to correct errors?
Methodology

  • preferred method: in-person
  • remote method employed when necessary due to time and cost restraints.
Sessions

  • individually engage in 10-15 minute usability test sessions
  • 30 minutes will be set aside for each session for the tester in anticipation of possible questions about the study, technical issues, participants running late, etc.

Iterations

Preference Test

Around this time, I received some feedback about the background color from my tutor and peers, I ran a preference test to validate this feedback.

With overwhelmingly clear results, I updated my prototype. I'm glad to have had the preference test results in hand - updating every page took much longer than expected and I may have given up halfway if I had not seen the data to prove this was absolutely the right choice.

Navigation Bars

  • Create uniform look on the top navigation bar by labeling all icons
  • Add more emphasis on selected state on bottom navigation
  • Add color to navigation bars to improve visuals
Filter Chips

  • Improve visuals by adding icons and colors
  • Declutter area above exercise results through the use of horizontal scroll
Journaling

  • Reduce cognitive load by dividing journaling prompts to multiple pages
  • Add color to text field to differentiate from prompt
